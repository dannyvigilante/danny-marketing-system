## THE BRIEF

I got tired of context switching.

Running multiple brands means your brain is always split вҖ” you're thinking strategy while writing copy while checking what went out last Tuesday. Every agency I looked at wanted a retainer for work I knew I could systematize. So I built the department I actually wanted.

This is a complete marketing department run by AI agents. Nine specialists that know their jobs, hand off to each other, and get smarter every cycle. It runs on [Claude](https://anthropic.com), uses [Notion](https://notion.so) as its operational nervous system, [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md) as its long-term brain, and a set of automations that mean the intelligence loop runs without me touching it every month.

This isn't a prompt wrapper. It's architecture.

**Ops Manager** вҖ” Traffic controller. Receives briefs, decomposes into tasks, routes to the right agent, monitors progress, escalates blockers. The only agent that talks to all the others.

**Chief Strategist** вҖ” Sets direction. Content pillars, campaign briefs, quarterly focus. Decides *what* the department does and *why*. Pulls from Researcher and Analyst intelligence.

**Content Strategist** вҖ” Bridges strategy and execution. Translates the Chief Strategist's direction into specific editorial calendars and content briefs. Manages the content calendar. Decides *what to create* and *when*.

**Researcher** вҖ” Competitive intelligence and market research. Scans the landscape for what's hot, what's saturated, and what gap exists. Powers the monthly intelligence scan.

**Analyst** вҖ” Performance data and insights. Tracks what worked, what didn't, and why. Feeds back into strategy so the system tightens over time.

**Copywriter** вҖ” Creates the work. Blog posts, social copy, email sequences, video scripts вҖ” everything across all formats and channels.

**SEO Specialist** вҖ” Optimizes for search without compromising voice. Keyword research, on-page structure, content gap analysis.

**QA Reviewer** вҖ” Quality gate before anything ships. Brand compliance scoring across 5 dimensions. Must hit вүҘ 4.0 to pass. Sends back to Copywriter with specific revision notes.

**Channel Manager** вҖ” Last mile. Takes approved content, formats for each platform, schedules, publishes, cross-posts.

Every agent loads the **GSD (Get Shit Done) protocol** вҖ” an autonomous execution framework that turns blockers into decisions instead of dead ends.

## THE BRAIN

Most AI systems are amnesiac. Every session starts from zero. This one doesn't.

The system runs on two memory layers that serve different functions:

### Notion вҖ” Operational Memory

The live nervous system. Everything that's *happening right now* lives here.

```
Marketing Department/
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Task Pipeline          вҶҗ What agents are working on
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Campaign Tracker       вҶҗ Campaign-level management
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Content Calendar       вҶҗ Publishing schedule across all brands
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Agent Learning Log     вҶҗ What each agent learned from each task
в””в”Җв”Җ Brand Knowledge Base   вҶҗ Facts, decisions, voice rules per brand
```

Notion is where the department operates. Tasks flow through it. Content status lives in it. Nothing ships without touching it.

### Obsidian вҖ” Institutional Memory

The long-term brain. Everything that *compounds over time* lives here.

```
Pace Gate Brain/
в”ңв”Җв”Җ marketing/
в”Ӯ   в”ңв”Җв”Җ content-ideas/        вҶҗ Intelligence briefs + content opportunities
в”Ӯ   в”ңв”Җв”Җ competitor-intel/     вҶҗ Ad-hoc competitor research
в”Ӯ   в”ңв”Җв”Җ brand-knowledge/      вҶҗ Audience and market research
в”Ӯ   в”ңв”Җв”Җ campaign-notes/       вҶҗ Performance analysis + learnings
в”Ӯ   в””в”Җв”Җ strategy/             вҶҗ Campaign briefs + quarterly direction
в”ңв”Җв”Җ writing/
в”Ӯ   в”ңв”Җв”Җ drafts/               вҶҗ All content produced, shipped or not
в”Ӯ   в””в”Җв”Җ research/             вҶҗ Source material and reference
в””в”Җв”Җ 00-inbox/                 вҶҗ Session logs + Monday briefings
```

Agents write to Obsidian after every significant task. Research goes into `content-ideas/`. Copy drafts go into `writing/drafts/`. Strategy decisions go into `marketing/strategy/`. Session summaries go into `00-inbox/`.

The result: a vault that grows with every session. In Obsidian's graph view, you can see the connections вҖ” intelligence briefs linking to brand pages, campaign notes linking to strategy docs, everything woven together over time.

**The principle:** Notion is the whiteboard. Obsidian is the library.

## THE INTELLIGENCE LOOP

The system runs a recurring intelligence cycle that keeps the department fed with fresh signal.

### Monthly Intelligence Scan

*Runs: Last Friday of every month at 8:00am*

The Researcher agent scans the cultural and competitive landscape for every active brand. Four research vectors per brand: what's trending, what competitors are publishing, what's missing (the gap), and what cultural moments create entry points.

Output for each brand:
- **What's Hot** вҖ” sourced, specific, no generic observations
- **What's Saturated** вҖ” the angles the audience is already tired of
- **Content Opportunities** вҖ” 5 differentiated content ideas with titles, channels, and timing

When the scan completes, the system automatically:
1. Creates the `рҹ“Ҡ Content Intelligence Brief вҖ” [Month YYYY]` in Notion
2. Saves `Intelligence-Brief-YYYY-MM.md` to Obsidian at `marketing/content-ideas/` with wikilinks for graph view
3. Creates **15 content calendar entries** in Notion вҖ” 5 per brand вҖ” with Copywriter-ready briefs and staggered publish dates
4. Sets a reminder for the following Monday at 8:00am

The content calendar goes from empty to 15 planned pieces, automatically, while you sleep.

### Monday Morning Briefing

*Runs: Every Monday at 8:08am*

The chief-of-staff brief. Pulls from Notion (task pipeline + content calendar), Obsidian (recent session notes + active drafts), and вҖ” when available вҖ” the latest intelligence scan.

If a scan ran in the past 7 days, the briefing leads with **Intelligence Scan Highlights**: the top opportunities per brand that are worth acting on that week. The brief then covers the task pipeline, what's publishing this week, and a scene-level writing prompt if you're working on long-form.

Saves to Notion, saves to Obsidian inbox, sets a 9:00am reminder.

```
Last Friday of month вҶ’ Intelligence Scan runs
                     вҶ’ 15 calendar entries created
                     вҶ’ Obsidian brief saved

Following Monday вҶ’ Briefing runs
                 вҶ’ Pulls scan highlights
                 вҶ’ Surfaces the week's priorities
                 вҶ’ You show up knowing what to move on
```

No manual handoff. No "remember to check the scan." The intelligence flows automatically to wherever decisions get made.

## CREATIVE REVIEW & DELIVERY

Content doesn't just get written вҖ” it gets reviewed against brand standards and published to channels.

### Review Layer (Canva)

Before anything ships, creative assets and copy live in Canva for review. This is the approval gate where content is checked visually and contextually вҖ” layout, brand alignment, copy in context. QA Reviewer scoring happens before this stage; Canva is for final eyes before distribution.

### Distribution Layer (Ayrshare)

Approved content publishes through Ayrshare for cross-platform distribution. Single publish, multiple channels. The Channel Manager handles formatting differences per platform (caption length, hashtag strategy, image dimensions) before hitting publish.

```
Copywriter drafts вҶ’ QA Reviewer scores (вүҘ 4.0 to pass)
                 вҶ’ Canva review (visual + contextual check)
                 вҶ’ Ayrshare publish (cross-platform distribution)
                 вҶ’ Analyst tracks performance
                 вҶ’ Learning fed back to Researcher + Strategist
```

The loop closes. Every piece of content that ships teaches the system something.

## BRAND MODULARITY

The system is brand-agnostic. One department, any number of brands.

Each brand lives in its own configuration directory:

```
brand-configs/
в””в”Җв”Җ your-brand/
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ brand-config.md         вҶҗ Core identity, positioning, mission
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ voice-profile.md        вҶҗ Tone, language patterns, what to avoid
    в”ңв”Җв”Җ audience-segments.md    вҶҗ Who you're talking to and why they care
    в””в”Җв”Җ channel-strategy.md     вҶҗ Where you show up and how
```

**Example вҖ” `brand-configs/your-brand/brand-config.md`:**

```markdown
# [Your Brand] вҖ” Brand Configuration

## Identity
What this brand is, what it stands for, what it's building toward.

## Positioning
What makes this brand different. Not what it does вҖ” what it *means*.

## Mission
The reason it exists beyond making money.

## Key Messages
The 3-5 things this brand always comes back to, no matter the format.

## Competitors to Monitor
Who's in the same space, what they're doing, where the gap is.

## Non-Negotiables
What this brand never does. Hard lines.
```

**Example вҖ” `brand-configs/your-brand/voice-profile.md`:**

```markdown
# [Your Brand] вҖ” Voice Profile

## Tone
Authoritative but not cold. Specific but not academic.
Talks to the audience like they're already smart.

## Language Patterns
Uses: [Examples of language that feels on-brand]
Avoids: [Examples of language that feels off-brand]

## Reference Points
The publications, creators, and cultural touchstones
that inform the brand's sensibility.

## Writing Tests
"Does this sound like something [Brand] would actually say?"
"Would [target reader] feel seen or talked at?"
```

Add a brand to the registry, load its config, and every agent immediately knows how to serve it. The Ops Manager routes correctly. The Copywriter hits the right voice. The QA Reviewer scores against the right rubric.

The Brand Registry in Notion tracks all active brands with their topics, audiences, competitors, and channels. The monthly intelligence scan automatically processes every active brand in the registry вҖ” you add a brand once, it enters the intelligence loop.

## THE PHILOSOPHY

Marketing departments fail for one of two reasons: they generate too much content with no strategy behind it, or they have great strategy that never gets executed because the production loop is too slow.

This system is designed to close both gaps at once. The intelligence layer (Researcher, Analyst, monthly scan) keeps the strategy grounded in what's actually happening in the market. The execution layer (Copywriter, SEO, QA, Channel Manager) makes sure ideas get produced, reviewed, and shipped without the whole thing falling on one person.

The memory layer вҖ” Notion for operations, Obsidian for institutional knowledge вҖ” means the system compounds. The first month it knows your brand. The sixth month it knows your brand's history, what worked, what didn't, and where the gaps are. That compounding is the actual value.

> The intelligence doesn't live in the prompt. It lives in the infrastructure.
