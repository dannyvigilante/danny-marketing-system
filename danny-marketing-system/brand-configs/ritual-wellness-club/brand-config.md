# Brand Config — The Ritual Wellness Club

## Brand Identity

**Full Name:** The Ritual Wellness Club
**Short Name:** The Ritual
**Tagline:** The Art of Wellness
**Instagram:** @TheRitualPDX
**Location:** Portland, Oregon (hosted at The Ritz-Carlton Portland)
**Founded:** 2025
**Entity Type:** Community wellness club (growth path: run club → wellness events → annual gala → non-profit/social enterprise)

## Mission

The Ritual is a wellness club rooted in The Art of Wellness — the practice of movement, recovery, and mindfulness, experienced through community. We design intentional spaces where people can build sustainable relationships with their health — not through performance or pressure, but through presence, consistency, and connection.

## Vision

To redefine wellness as a shared, elevated, and repeatable practice — where anyone, regardless of background or experience, can participate in rituals that support a more balanced, connected life.

## Brand Positioning

**The Ritual is silent luxury wellness.**

Like Bottega Veneta or Hermès, The Ritual doesn't need to announce itself. The quality, intention, and craft are evident to those who understand.

**The Spyker C8 Lens:** Engineered with obsessive care. Luxury that earns its place. Rare and distinctive. Timeless and relevant. For people who know.

## Core Pillars

1. **Movement as Practice** — Consistency over intensity
2. **Recovery as Essential** — Rest is non-negotiable
3. **Community as Infrastructure** — Built connection, not just gathered people
4. **Intention Over Intensity** — Presence over grind
5. **Access for All** — Inclusive, barrier-reduced wellness

## Competitive Differentiation

The Ritual occupies a unique position: **luxury wellness with genuine accessibility.** Most wellness brands either go premium-exclusive or mass-market-accessible. The Ritual delivers premium experience and inclusive access. The Ritz-Carlton setting signals quality; the "you don't have to be fast to belong" ethos signals welcome.

## Brand Guardrails

**Never:**
- Use hustle culture language ("no pain no gain," "beast mode," "grind")
- Position wellness as achievement or competition
- Exclude based on fitness level, body type, or experience
- Over-explain the luxury positioning — if you have to explain it, you're doing it wrong
- Use generic wellness stock imagery (smiling person with smoothie)

**Always:**
- Lead with community and belonging
- Let the quality speak through specificity, not superlatives
- Center real people and real moments
- Maintain the tension between elevated and accessible
- Use Portland as a character, not just a location
