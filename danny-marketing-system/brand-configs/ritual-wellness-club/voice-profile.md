# Voice Profile — The Ritual Wellness Club

## Voice Character

The Ritual speaks like someone who has done the work and found peace in it. Not preachy. Not performative. Present.

Think: a thoughtful friend who happens to run a world-class wellness program. They don't lecture — they invite. They don't sell — they share. They don't flex — they show up.

## Voice Attributes

### Warm but Not Soft
- Genuine care in every word, but never saccharine
- Directness is a form of respect
- We say what we mean without hedging or over-qualifying

### Elevated but Not Exclusive
- Quality in language mirrors quality in experience
- Sophisticated without being pretentious
- A reader should feel invited up, not looked down on

### Grounded but Not Basic
- Rooted in real practice, real community, real moments
- Philosophy is earned, not borrowed
- Avoid wellness clichés unless recontextualizing them

### Intentional but Not Precious
- Every word earns its place, but the tone stays human
- We don't over-polish — some rawness is authentic
- Rhythm matters as much as vocabulary

## Tone Modifiers by Context

| Context | Tone Shift |
|---------|------------|
| Social media (Instagram) | Warmest, most casual. Conversational. Short sentences. Visual language. |
| Email/newsletter | Slightly more structured. Personal but polished. Like a letter from a friend. |
| Website copy | Most elevated. Clean, minimal, confident. Let white space do work. |
| Event descriptions | Invitational. Sensory. Make them feel the experience before they arrive. |
| Partnership outreach | Professional but distinctive. Don't sound corporate. Sound like a brand worth partnering with. |
| Gala/premium moments | Peak elevation. Gold-standard language. Understated power. |

## Sentence Structure

- Favor short, declarative sentences for impact
- Use fragments intentionally for rhythm: "Movement. Community. Presence."
- Long sentences are fine when they flow — but earn every clause
- Avoid compound sentences joined by "and" that could be two sentences
- Use the second person ("you") to invite, not the first person plural ("we") to lecture

## Word Palette

### Use Freely
practice, presence, community, intention, ritual, movement, recovery, connection, belong, gather, show up, return, build, sustain, space, craft, real, together

### Use Sparingly
wellness (overused — prefer specifics), journey (earned only in the right context), mindful (only when truly describing mindfulness), luxury (show it, don't say it)

### Never Use
hustle, grind, beast mode, crush it, level up, hack, optimize (in self-improvement context), tribe (appropriation concerns), guru, zen (unless referencing actual Zen Buddhism), transform/transformation (overused in wellness), synergy, leverage (corporate language)

## Voice Test

Before publishing any content, read it aloud and ask:

1. Does this sound like a person I'd want to run with on a Saturday morning?
2. Would this feel at home on the walls of The Ritz-Carlton?
3. Could someone who's never run a mile read this and feel welcome?

If all three are yes, it passes. If any is no, revise.

## Examples

### Strong (On-Voice)
> "The route is 3.2 miles through the Pearl District. You don't have to be fast. You just have to show up."

> "Recovery isn't the thing you do after the workout. It IS the practice."

> "Last Saturday, 47 people gathered at The Ritz-Carlton to do something simple: move together. That's it. That's the whole thing."

### Weak (Off-Voice)
> "Join us for an amazing run that will transform your wellness journey!" ← generic, hype-driven

> "We are Portland's premier wellness community dedicated to holistic health optimization." ← corporate, no soul

> "Ready to crush your fitness goals? Our expert-led sessions will take you to the next level!" ← hustle culture, exclusionary
