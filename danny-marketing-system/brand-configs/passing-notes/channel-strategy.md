# Channel Strategy — Passing Notes Through a Glass Wall

## Primary Channel: Instagram

**Role:** Brand awareness, community building, excerpt-sharing. Where the book finds new readers.

**Content Mix:**
- Quote cards (2-3/week): Sharp excerpts from the book over clean design backgrounds
- Personal posts (1-2/week): Danny sharing context, behind-the-book stories, the anchor story
- Carousels: "5 things I wish my parents knew" style educational content
- Reels: Short-form video of Danny speaking directly to camera about key themes
- Stories: Real-time reflections, reader messages, book events

**Tone:** Danny's voice, unfiltered. Conversational but pointed. Systems-thinking made accessible.

**Growth Strategy:**
- Engage with adoption community accounts
- Share in relevant hashtag communities (#TransracialAdoption, #AdoptiveParenting)
- Cross-post to Facebook adoption groups (where the primary audience lives)
- Collaborate with transracial adoptee creators

---

## Secondary Channel: Facebook Groups

**Role:** Direct access to the primary audience. White adoptive parents congregate in Facebook groups.

**Approach:**
- Not advertising — participating. Danny or a trusted ambassador shares genuinely.
- Lead with the anchor story. It opens doors without defensiveness.
- Respond to questions and discussions about transracial parenting
- Offer the book as a resource, not a product

**Key Groups to Target:**
- Transracial adoption support groups
- Adoptive parent communities
- Multiracial family groups
- Book clubs focused on race and identity

**Rules:**
- Never spam. Never drop links without context.
- Share the story first. The book link follows naturally.
- Engage with members' questions — build trust before asking for anything.

---

## Tertiary Channel: LinkedIn

**Role:** Thought leadership positioning. Where Danny's professional network intersects with the book's themes.

**Content Mix:**
- Long-form posts connecting book themes to professional/institutional contexts
- "The organizational behavior of a transracial family" angle
- Systems-thinking applied to corporate DEI, education, military
- Book excerpts positioned as professional insight, not personal memoir

**Tone:** Professional register but still Danny's voice. MBA meets memoir.

**Frequency:** 1-2 posts/week during active marketing pushes

---

## Emerging Channel: TikTok

**Role:** Reach younger audiences and the transracial adoptee community.

**Content Mix:**
- Danny speaking directly to camera (30-60 seconds)
- "Things my White family never understood" series
- Reading excerpts with commentary
- Responding to adoption content on the platform

**Tone:** Most casual. Raw. Authentic. TikTok rewards honesty, and Danny has it.

---

## Email/Newsletter (Future)

**Role:** Deeper engagement with committed readers. Where the book's themes expand.

**Content:** Monthly reflections on themes from the book, reader stories, framework applications, updates on the next project.

---

## Outreach Channel: Direct to Organizations

**Role:** Bulk sales and speaking opportunities.

**Targets:**
- Adoption agencies (training material)
- University diversity offices
- Corporate DEI programs
- Faith-based adoption organizations
- Libraries and book clubs

**Approach:** Personalized outreach with the anchor story, review copies, speaking offer.

---

## Cross-Channel Rules

1. The anchor story is the universal hook — use it everywhere, adapt the framing
2. Never soften the message for comfort — the discomfort is the point
3. Danny's voice is the constant — channel format changes, voice doesn't
4. Utility framing for parents, depth framing for intellectuals, recognition framing for community
5. Marketing the book IS the book's work — every post plants a seed
