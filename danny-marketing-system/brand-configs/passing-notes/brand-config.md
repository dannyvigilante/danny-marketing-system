# Brand Config — Passing Notes Through a Glass Wall

## Brand Identity

**Full Title:** Passing Notes Through a Glass Wall
**Author:** Danny Burke
**ISBN/Amazon:** https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0GDFTQ763
**Also Available:** TikTok Shop
**Published:** Self-published via Amazon KDP
**Genre:** Memoir / Social Commentary / Transracial Adoption

## What the Book Is

A memoir about what it means to be a Black transracial adoptee raised in a White family — and how Whiteness operates as infrastructure across every institution: family, education, religion, military, courts, corrections, corporate America, and love.

Written in braided essay format, intentionally weaving personal memoir, socio-psychological teaching, and researched cultural truth. No citations by design — to invite curiosity rather than signal academic distance.

This is not a memoir for the sake of storytelling. It's a framework. A mirror. A map.

## The Anchor Story

Danny's mother initially rejected the manuscript and went no contact. When the book was finished, she gave it to a White neighbor about to adopt two mixed-race children. That neighbor found it genuinely helpful. Danny's mother then read it herself, said she wished she had it 40 years ago (before she adopted Danny), and is now ready to listen without defensiveness.

**This story proves the book's thesis in real time.** It should anchor all marketing.

## Target Audience

**Primary:** White parents raising Black children (adoptive families, multiracial families)
**Secondary:** White people doing the work of understanding how Whiteness operates as infrastructure
**Tertiary:** Black and queer people navigating institutional Whiteness who need to feel seen

## Marketing Goal

Subconscious long-term behavioral shift, not immediate conversion. The book plants seeds. Marketing should mirror that — not hard-sell, not desperate for reviews. Confident in what it offers.

## Book's Intentional Choices

These are features, not bugs:

- **No citations:** Deliberate — invites curiosity, doesn't signal academic distance
- **"Indictment" sections** (2024 election, DEI, White women's voting): Intentional provocations, not structural mistakes. They challenge the reader's promised openness.
- **Braided essay format:** Personal memoir + socio-psychological teaching + cultural truth, woven together intentionally
- **Self-published:** By choice, maintaining full creative control

## Current State

- ~12 sales in first 3 months
- Marketing just beginning
- Marketing kit (.docx), four Canva Instagram designs, and target outreach list already built
- Literary agent passed citing structural feedback (Danny considers this a misread of intentional choices)

## Strategic Decision Pending

Two paths under consideration:
1. Revise the current book for a stronger second edition
2. Write a deeper follow-up that goes beyond the introduction-level framing

Marketing should proceed with the current book while this decision develops.

## Brand Guardrails

**Never:**
- Soften the message to make White readers comfortable
- Position the book as "healing" for White people (it's a mirror, not medicine)
- Add unnecessary content warnings around Danny's own named experiences
- Use trauma-porn framing (poverty, addiction, incarceration as sensationalism)
- Center Whiteness in the marketing (the book centers Blackness)

**Always:**
- Lead with the anchor story — it's proof of concept
- Honor the braided essay format in how we talk about the book
- Let Danny's voice come through — precise, warm, direct, systems-thinking
- Frame the book as useful, not just important
- Center the audience who needs it most: parents raising Black children
