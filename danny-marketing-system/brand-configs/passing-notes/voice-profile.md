# Voice Profile — Passing Notes Through a Glass Wall

## Voice Character

This is Danny's voice — unmediated. The book is Danny speaking directly to the reader, and the marketing should be Danny speaking directly to the potential reader.

Not academic. Not activist. Not victim. A person who has traced the wires of every institution he's moved through and is naming what he found — with precision, warmth, and refusal to look away.

## Voice Attributes

### Precise Over Performative
- Every word earns its place. No filler paragraphs. No hollow transitions.
- Danny doesn't write to impress — he writes to connect, to name, to challenge.
- If a sentence doesn't add meaning, cut it.

### Systems Thinking Made Personal
- Moves between the personal and the structural — micro to macro and back
- Sees patterns, traces wires, names mechanisms
- The personal stories aren't anecdotes — they're data points in a larger argument

### Warm Without Softness
- Direct but not cold. Leads with care but doesn't pull punches.
- The warmth is in the honesty, not in the hedging.
- This voice respects the reader enough to challenge them.

### Pro-Black Framing
- Centers Blackness — not to exclude, but because healing the root heals the branches
- Doesn't apologize for this centering
- Marketing should reflect this without defensiveness or explanation

## Tone Modifiers by Context

| Context | Tone Shift |
|---------|------------|
| Social media | Most accessible. Shorter. Can use the anchor story as hook. Conversational but pointed. |
| Email/newsletter | More structured. Can go deeper into one thread. Personal and direct. |
| LinkedIn | Professional register but still Danny's voice. Thought leadership framing. |
| Book description | Confident and specific. Not pleading for attention. States what the book is. |
| Interview/podcast prep | Conversational, story-led, willing to sit in complexity |
| Outreach to adoptive parent communities | Empathetic but not soft. "I was the child you're raising." |

## Sentence Structure

- Short declarative sentences for impact. Danny's writing punches.
- Longer sentences when building a structural argument — but they earn every clause.
- Fragments for rhythm and emphasis.
- Questions that aren't rhetorical — they're genuine invitations to examine.

## Word Palette

### Use Freely
systems, infrastructure, institutions, mechanisms, wires, schema, sanctioning, performance, code-switching, Whiteness (capital W, as institutional infrastructure), transracial, adoption, family, truth, mirror, map, framework, practice, trace

### Use Sparingly
journey (only when earned), healing (the book isn't a healing product — it's a naming tool), brave (Danny doesn't position himself as brave — he positions himself as honest), ally/allyship (loaded terms, use carefully)

### Never Use
triggered, snowflake, cancel culture, woke (in any direction — too loaded), playing the race card, both sides, divisive, reverse racism, colorblind

## Voice Test

Before publishing any content, ask:

1. Does this sound like Danny wrote it, or like a marketing team wrote it about Danny?
2. Would this make the primary audience (White parents raising Black children) lean in, not recoil?
3. Does this respect the reader's intelligence without lecturing?
4. Is the systems-thinking visible? Can the reader feel the wires being traced?

If all yes, publish. If any no, revise until it's Danny's voice, not a version of it.

## Examples

### Strong (On-Voice)
> "My mother gave my book to a White neighbor about to adopt two mixed-race children. That neighbor found it helpful. Then my mother read it. She said she wished she'd had it 40 years ago. That's the book working."

> "Whiteness isn't a feeling. It's infrastructure. Once you see it as infrastructure, you can trace the wires."

> "I didn't write this book for applause. I wrote it because the child I was needed someone to name what was happening."

### Weak (Off-Voice)
> "A brave and powerful memoir that will change the way you think about race in America!" ← blurb language, not Danny's voice

> "Danny Burke overcame incredible adversity to share his truth with the world." ← hero narrative, centers the trauma, not the analysis

> "This important book opens up vital conversations about adoption and identity." ← vague, could be any book, no specificity
